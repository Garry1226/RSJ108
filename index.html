How to mask a subject in photoshop?

Assembly time: 15 minutes- 30 minutes
Awesome rating: 100% awesome

Are you wondering how to mask a subject in a photo you took in photoshop? . Are you afraid to mess it up when you try to do it yourself? Follow the steps and you'll be able to make your vision come to life

  • Things You'll Need

  • Raw photo of subject
  • Access to photoshop
  • A Go Getter attitude
  • Willingness to fail

  1. first open up your photoshop app.

    There should be an app logo with the letters PS on the front.

  2. Click create new file and choose the dimensions that you would like to edit in.

    This is an important step because you have to consider what length and width your photo is. also when choosing the dimensions make sure you title your photoshop file.

    All labeled within the white circles.

  3. Now we are going to add our image, click file at the top of your monitor and click place embedded. .

    Now find wherever you placed your raw photo file.

  4. Most Important Step!!!

    Now that your image is placed we are now going to right click on the image in the task bar and click (convert to smart object).

  5. Image Cutting Time.

    There should be a tool on the left side task bar and it looks like a pen and a dotted line box and that is going to be the tool you use to cut out the subject in your photo.

  6. Selecting your subject.

    Now with that tool selected. Hover over your subject and it should turn pink.Then click on the image and a task bar shold pop up. Now click the box with a circle in the middle and it should say (create new mask).
